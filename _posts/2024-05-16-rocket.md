# <span style="color:#fff;">Goals</span>

- Break the sound barrier
- Reach an altitude of 2000m
- Successful tracking and recovery
- Learn how to build subminimum rockets

<br><br>

# <span style="color:#fff;">Fall 2024</span>
Around that time, couple months after my successful minimum diameter launch I've came up with an idea of building subminimum rocket - where 
motor casing acts as a rocket airframe. Originally I was planning to use 40mm aluminium tube, but I settled on 45mm instead due to lack of 
space to contain all of the necessary electronics. Nozzle was made from steel and endcap was made from aluminium.

Recovery
</p>
For recovery I used 30cm nylon parachute and about 2.5 meters of kevlar cord. Due to launch site limitations, descent rate was about 11m/s. Flight computer responsible for recovery was Arecorder, well-known in Polish Rocketry Association, with a lot of flight heritage. I used two ejection charges, one for main seperation and one as a backup. I also made sure that recovery system works.

# <span style="color:#fff;">September 6</span>
On the first weekend of September an event called "Festiwal Meteora" took place on Błędowska Desert in Poland. On saturday, 6th, I made last finishing touches to the rocket, for example little correction of motor endcap. Rest of that day i spent on documenting whole event. When I got back to hotel room the problems started. Firstly, the flight computer did not recognize memory card, which is a must in order to arm the computer. Additionaly, while assembling the motor, the nozzle got stuck. I wasted 1 hour on trying to fix these things, which didn't bring any succes. 

# <span style="color:#fff;">September 7 - Launch Day</span>
When I woke up at 7 a.m I quickly tried for the last time to unstuck the nozzle - this time succesfuly. The memory card problem was solved with my friend bringing a spare one to the launch site. At roughly 9 a.m I arrived at the desert and started preparing rocket for launch and made ejection charges. - it took quite long, so slowly I was getting a rush fever. Then set up launch tower and prepared ignition system. I quickly packed the chute and It was ready for launch.
